Gallery layout — where do things go?

28 × 24 m room (top-down). Enter from the front (bottom of each plan). Pick a layout.

A

Linear gallery walk

Single long aisle front-to-back. Panels line both walls. Floor exhibits down the center. Arcades + desk as one interactive cluster on the left. Classic museum pace.

B

Central spine with side alcoves

Long central aisle. Floor exhibits march down the spine (pedestal → globe → rabbit hole). Arcades and desk tucked into a left alcove so they don't dominate. TV anchors the back wall.

C

Themed zones (quadrants)

Room split in four: research panels + reading (left-back), interactive/arcade (left-front), media/TV (right-back), artifacts + rabbit hole (right-front). Visitors wander; no prescribed path.

My recommendation: B (central spine) — gives clear guided walk with sightlines to every exhibit, keeps gaming content present but tucked aside so gallery atmosphere dominates.
