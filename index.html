Chapter 1: The Digital Narrative

Digital narratives are stories that leverage computational media to create meaning. Unlike traditional linear narratives, they can be interactive, procedural, and responsive to their audience.

The Centre for Digital Narrative at the University of Bergen studies how these new forms of storytelling shape culture, identity, and knowledge.

Chapter 2: Interactive Fiction

From Zork to Twine, interactive fiction has evolved from text adventures into a rich medium for artistic expression. Readers become co-authors, making choices that shape the narrative.

CDN researchers explore how choice architecture in interactive fiction affects reader engagement and emotional investment.

Chapter 3: Generative Storytelling

With the rise of large language models, stories can now be generated collaboratively between humans and machines. This raises fundamental questions about authorship, creativity, and narrative agency.

CDN's research examines the boundary between human and machine storytelling — where does the author end and the algorithm begin?

Chapter 4: Virtual Worlds as Narrative Spaces

From MMORPGs to VR experiences, virtual worlds are narrative environments where stories emerge from player interaction, environmental design, and social dynamics.

This gallery itself is an experiment in spatial storytelling — how does navigating a 3D space change the way you engage with research?
